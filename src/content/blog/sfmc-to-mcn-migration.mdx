If you run Marketing Cloud Engagement and you've sat through a Salesforce QBR in the last year, you've heard about Marketing Cloud Next. Probably framed two ways at once: *"this is the future of the platform"* and *"don't worry, nothing is being sunset."* Both statements are true. That's exactly why teams are confused.

**The short answer before the long one:** for most MCE orgs right now, a full migration is the wrong project. Getting your data ready for Data Cloud is the right one.

## What Marketing Cloud Next Actually Is

Marketing Cloud Next (MCN) was announced at Connections in June 2025. It is **not** a new version of MCE. It's a rebuild of Salesforce's marketing stack on the core platform, with Data 360 (Data Cloud) as the data foundation and Agentforce as the native AI layer. It folds the acquisition history — ExactTarget, Pardot, Datorama, Evergage — into one application that covers both B2B and B2C.

## What Salesforce Is (and Isn't) Saying

Salesforce is deliberate about the word *convergence*. There is no sunset date for MCE. Email Studio, Journey Builder, Content Builder, and Automation Studio continue to ship and receive updates. Nobody is forcing a cutover.

The mechanism is the **Engagement+ path**: everything in your current MCE org stays intact, and the on-core capabilities (Marketing Cloud Advanced, Data Cloud, Agentforce) are added alongside it on a consumption basis. You build new messaging and flows in MCN and invoke your existing Journey Builder journeys from within them — a daisy-chain, not a lift-and-shift.

Wrong first question, but let's answer it anyway. It depends on what your org actually looks like.

## The Part Nobody Puts on the Slide

Whatever timeline you pick, the gating factor is the same: **your data**.

Data Cloud's unified profile is only as good as what feeds it. If your subscriber keys are inconsistent across business units, your data extensions are undocumented, and your Marketing Cloud Connect sync has been quietly half-broken since the agency that built it left — identity resolution won't fix that. It will produce confident-looking unified profiles built on garbage, and every AI feature downstream inherits the problem.

Which is why the pragmatic sequence for an MCE org in 2026 looks like this:
